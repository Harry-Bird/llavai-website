# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-11 (auto-drafted from site copy at launch; review pending)*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Llavai is an AI rental concierge that helps expats win Barcelona flats — it surfaces the best new listings first, and on Pro, "Julia" calls the estate agents in native Spanish within ~60 seconds and books the viewings.
**What it does:** Llavai watches the Barcelona rental market, scores every new listing 0–100 for appeal, and shows subscribers only the ones worth their time with the agency's number one tap away. On the Pro plan, Julia (the AI voice agent) calls agencies within ~60 seconds of a listing going live, presents the tenant's profile in native Spanish, and books viewings around their calendar.
**Product category:** AI rental concierge / apartment-hunting service (customers search things like "how to rent in Barcelona as a foreigner", "rent apartment Barcelona expat" — they search the *problem*, not the category).
**Product type:** B2C subscription SaaS (Essential) + AI-powered concierge service (Pro, application-gated).
**Business model:** Monthly subscription via Stripe. Free 3-day trial of Essential, no payment details at signup, every account confirmed personally. Essential €19/mo (self-serve feed + one-tap calling). Pro €185/mo (Julia calls + books; "every application is reviewed personally — you only pay after you're approved"). Launched 2026-06-11.

## Target Audience
**Target customers:** Expats and internationals moving to (or within) Barcelona who don't speak fluent Spanish — professionals with steady income, couples, families, students. Site is trilingual EN/ES/UA, so Ukrainian newcomers are an explicit audience segment.
**Decision-makers:** The renter themselves (B2C; sometimes a couple deciding together).
**Primary use case:** Winning a long-term rental flat in Barcelona's speed-driven market without being fluent in Spanish or glued to Idealista alerts.
**Jobs to be done:**
- "Get me into the agent's diary before the other 40 applicants" (speed)
- "Represent me properly in Spanish so agents take me seriously" (language/credibility)
- "Filter the noise — only show me flats actually worth my time" (curation)
- "Take the soul-crushing call grind off my plate" (emotional relief / rejection fatigue)
**Use cases:**
- Newly arrived expat with no NIE/nómina yet who needs their profile presented well
- Busy professional who can't drop work calls to phone agencies within 30 seconds
- Ukrainian or other non-EN/ES-native newcomer navigating the market in a third language

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** "The search doesn't happen on Idealista. It happens in the thirty seconds after the alert hits your phone. Miss that window and it's gone. And if Spanish isn't your first language — you're already behind before you've dialled."
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Idealista alerts tell everyone at once — the alert is the starting gun, not an advantage
- Listings bury the facts that matter; good and bad flats look the same at a glance
- Calling agencies requires confident, fast, native-sounding Spanish
**What it costs them:** Lost flats they could afford; hours of refreshing and dead-end calls; paying over the odds or settling for worse neighbourhoods; weeks of temporary accommodation.
**Emotional tension:** Rejection fatigue, the anxiety of "competing in a sport nobody trained you for", feeling like a second-class applicant because of language.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Relocation agents / personal flat-hunters — falls short because they're expensive, human-scale (can't call within 60 seconds of every listing), and often serve corporate moves, not ordinary renters.
**Secondary:** Idealista saved-search alerts + DIY calling — falls short because everyone gets the same alert simultaneously, the listings are cluttered, and the renter still has to make the Spanish call themselves, fast.
**Indirect:** Facebook/WhatsApp expat groups, walking neighbourhoods, "a friend who speaks Spanish" — falls short because it's slow, unscalable, and dependent on favours.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Julia calls agencies within ~60 seconds of a listing going live — faster than any human service
- Native Barcelona Spanish, professional tone; presents the tenant dossier (contract, salary, references, guarantor) so agents take the applicant seriously
- Appeal score (0–100): who's behind it /25, presentation /20, value for money /20, features /20, freshness /15; only listings above 60 make the cut
- Everything tracked in one place — calls, WhatsApp, follow-ups, who ghosted you ("No more spreadsheets")
- Network intelligence that compounds: booking patterns, agency responsiveness, neighbourhood speed ("smarter over time — without ever sharing your personal data")
**How we do it differently:** AI voice agent + scoring pipeline instead of either raw alerts (too noisy, too slow) or human concierges (too expensive, too slow).
**Why that's better:** Speed is the entire game in Barcelona; only software answers an alert in 60 seconds, and only a native-sounding voice gets the applicant taken seriously.
**Why customers choose us:** "You stop losing flats to faster applicants" — without needing to be faster, luckier, or more fluent yourself.

**Anti-persona:** Fluent Spanish speakers with flexible daytime availability; people hunting rooms/flatshares or short-term stays; anyone renting outside Barcelona; bargain-hunters unwilling to pay anything for an edge.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Losing flat after flat to faster applicants; alert fatigue; draining, awkward Spanish phone calls; Idealista clutter.
**Pull:** "When the alert lands in your inbox, Llavai is already dialling"; viewings appear booked in your inbox; only the standout listings reach you.
**Habit:** Idealista alerts are free and feel like they should be enough; "I'll just be faster next time"; distrust of paying for something you can theoretically do yourself.
**Anxiety:** Will an AI misrepresent me to an agent? Is €185 worth it before I've seen it work? Is this legit? (Countered by: personal account confirmation, pay-after-approval on Pro, no card at signup, founder's name and email on the page.)
